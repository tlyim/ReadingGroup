Chen et al (2015 JAR)

Several remarks

Chen et al (2015) vs. D'Souza et al's (2010)

  • Developed a disaggregation quality measure (DQ) by counting nonmissing data items in company annual reports as reported by Compustat
    • As opposed to Dâ€™Souza et al's (2010) disclosure ratio measure concerning how comprehensive the disclosure in earnings announcements is compared to that in the SEC filings (based on line items reported in Compustat Preliminary History and Unrestated Quarterly databases).

Recognition and Disclosure

  • The DQ measure covers items in the footnotes, besides those in the financial statements.
    • Hence, make no distinction between recognition (FS items) and disclosure (fn items)
    • (?) Does this mean a research opportunity to devise measures for FS-DQ and fn-DQ?

“All” Compustat firms

  • Applicable to the universe of Compustat firms (excluding financial and utility firms due to different Compustat Balancing Models and also foreign firms)
    • not just some subset of firms (eg, large) or only part of the annual report (eg, narrative reporting in MD&A)
    • (?) Research opportunity to devise measures for financial firms (eg, banks)?

Suggestion for future research

  • Chen et al (2015): Relation between DQ measure and audit quality, and other aspects of financial reporting quality
    • (?) more disaggregation means the need to check each disaggregated disclosure
    • (?) Higher audit fees (because more work to do and higher litigation cost as more items subject to audit failure)

Random thought

  • (?) In the investment decision to choose a stock from two competing stocks from different industries (thus, with different disclosure practices in line with the two industries),

    • How to factor in the difference in their disclosure qualities? (eg, how to trade off disclosure quality and stock price?)

  • (?) Weight the importance of a missing item in an industry by its frequency in appearing in financial reports in that industry? (ie, penalize more if the missing item is rarely missing in that industry).

    • Is this different/better than simply adjusting for industry FEs uniformly?
